For example, consider the two systems
\begin{align*} x +y \amp= 2 \\ x +1.001y \amp =2 \end{align*}
\begin{align*} x+y \amp= 2\\ x+1.001y \amp= 2.001 \end{align*}
which obtain vastly different answers given the small change in the target. The corresponding matrix is ill-conditioned and can give different solutions with technology.
