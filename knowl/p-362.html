The volume of the parallelepiped is the area of its base times its height 16 . The base is the parallelogram with sides \(\vb\) and \(\vc\text{.}\) Its area is the length of its base, which is \(|\vb|\text{,}\) times its height, which is \(|\vc|\,\sin\theta\text{.}\) (Drop a perpendicular from the head of \(\vc\) to the line containing \(\vb\)). Here \(\theta\) is the angle between \(\vb\) and \(\vc\text{.}\) So the area of the base is \(|\vb|\,|\vc|\,\sin\theta= |\vb\times\vc|\text{,}\) by property 2 of the cross product.
