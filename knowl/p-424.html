Let \(A\) be a \(2\times 2\) real matrix with a complex eigenvalue \(\lambda\) and let \(\vec{v}\) be a corresponding eigenvector. Then
\begin{equation*} A=CBC^{-1} \end{equation*}
where and \(C = \left[\begin{matrix} | \amp | \\ Re(v) \amp Im(v) \\ | \amp | \end{matrix}\right]\) and \(B=\left[ \begin{matrix} Re(\lambda) \amp Im(\lambda) \\-Im(\lambda) \amp Re(\lambda) \end{matrix}\right]\) See Rotation Scaling Theorem in Section 5.5 of Interactive Linear Algebra. 9
