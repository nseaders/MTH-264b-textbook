Here are some important observations.
  • The vectors \(\va\times\vb\) and \(\vb\times\va\) are not the same! In fact \(\vb\times\va=-\va\times\vb\text{.}\) We shall see in Theorem Theorem 3.26 below that this was not a fluke.
  • The vector \(\va\times\vb\) has dot product zero with both \(\va\) and \(\vb\text{.}\) So the vector \(\va\times\vb\) is prependicular to both \(\va\) and \(\vb\text{.}\) We shall see in Theorem Theorem 3.26 below that this was also not a fluke.
